# Secret Sauce Listening Checklist

Use this for the first real listening pass after the current build.

## Test Sources

1. Dry vocal idea or phone-recorded vocal.
2. Melody loop or sample chop.
3. 808 or bass accent.
4. Drum loop.
5. Transition sound, riser, fill, or ear-candy moment.

## First Pass

For each source:

1. Load Secret Sauce.
2. Start on `Self Sample Sauce`.
3. Move `Sauce` from 25 to 75.
4. Try all 12 flavors.
5. Toggle the six performance buttons:
   `Freeze`, `Reverse`, `Repeat`, `Dropout`, `Tape Stop`, `Throw`.

## What To Listen For

Mark anything that stands out:

- Too quiet or too loud when enabled.
- Clicks when chops, repeats, dropouts, or buttons happen.
- `Sauce` knob gets intense too fast.
- `Sauce` knob does not do enough.
- Flavor modes sound too similar.
- A flavor is clearly useful and should be pushed harder.
- A flavor feels gimmicky or not musical.
- `Throw` is too short, too long, too quiet, or too messy.
- `Reverse` feels weak, late, or unnatural.
- `Tape Stop` is too subtle, too dark, or too fake.
- Low end gets muddy on 808/bass.
- High end gets harsh on vocals.
- Stereo width feels too wide or phasey.

## Best Feedback To Capture

The most useful notes are concrete:

- Which source was playing.
- Which flavor was selected.
- Approximate `Sauce` amount.
- Which button was on.
- What sounded good or bad.

Example:

```text
Vocal loop, Reverse Pull, Sauce around 60, Reverse on:
reverse is cool but arrives too late and gets cloudy.
```
